Coordination and paired clauses
===============================
{: .no_toc}

Coordination refers to how two or more words, phrases, or clauses can be joined together, often with a conjunction, such as “and” or “but”. Different conjunctions are used to express different relationships between the elements, e.g. cause-and-effect or a sequence of events.

Coordination without a conjunction
----------------------------------

In English, the conjunction “and” can join up clauses that do not necessarily depend on each other grammatically, but which point back to the subject of the sentence:

> She is a doctor, **and** \[she\] has worked for two years in Hong Kong.

However, a conjunction would not be necessary here in Teochew, as the same meaning can be conveyed by simple juxtaposition:

> I1 si7 ui1seng1, tsoih5 no7-ni5 to7 Hiang1kang2 tso2kang1.
>
> I1 si7 ui1sêng1, zoin5 no7-ni5 do7 Hiang1gang2 zo2gang1.
>
> 伊 是 醫生，前 二年 在 香港 做工。
>
> She is doctor, previous two-years in Hong-Kong work.

Conjunctive (“and”)
-------------------

### *ka’4* • *gah4* • 甲/佮 • “and”, “with”

Nouns and noun phrases are joined with *ka’4*:

> Ua2 siang7 hi6huar1 kai7 tsher1kuaih2 si7 nek4oih2 **ka’4** ni7kue2.
>
> Ua2 siang7 hi6huan1 gai7 cên1guain2 si7 nêg4oin2 **gah4** ni7guê2.
>
> 我 上 喜歡 個 青果 是 肉眼 **甲** 奶果。
>
> I most like REL fruit is longan **and** papaya.
>
> My favorite fruits are longan and papaya.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> I1 zik4zik8 tuar1tuar1 thoi2-tiang7si6 **ka’4** seung6-tiang7nau2 tiar7.
>
> I1 rig4rig8 duan1duan1 toi2-diang7si6 **gah4** seng6-diang7nao2 tian7.
>
> 伊 日日 單單 睇 電視 **甲** 耍 電腦 爾。
>
> He daily only watch-TV **and** play-computer only.
>
> The only thing he does all day is to watch TV and play computer games.

*Ka’4* also means “with”, “for” (see [“Passives and ‘give’ constructions”](pages/passives.md)), but in these cases is a preposition/coverb, not a conjunction:

> Ua2 e7-loi6pai7 **ka’4** pheng7iou2 kheu2 Hiang1kang2.
>
> Ua2 ê7-loi6bai7 **gah4** pêng7iou2 ke2 Hiang1gang2.
>
> 我 下禮拜 **甲** 朋友 去 香港。
>
> I next-week **with** friend go Hong-Kong.
>
> I’m going to Hong Kong next week with a friend.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> I1 **ka’4** uang2 teng5 ki1phie3 lou7.
>
> I1 **gah4** uang2 dêng5 gi1piê3 lou7.
>
> 伊 **甲** 阮 訂 機票 路。
>
> He **for** we-excl. book plane-ticket PERF.
>
> He has already booked the plane tickets for us.

Disjunctive (“or”)
------------------

### *a1si7* • *a1si7* • 阿是 • “or”

> Leu2 ai2 lim1 ko1pi5 **a1si7** te5?
>
> Le2 ai2 lim1 go1bi5 **a1si7** dê5?
>
> 汝 愛 啉 咖啡 **阿是** 茶？
>
> You want drink coffee **or** tea?
>
> Do you want to drink coffee or tea?

### *hok4* • *hog4* • 或 • “or”

Contrast and concession (“but”)
-------------------------------

Linking together two thoughts that contrast with each other, e.g. that oppose each other or which are unexpected and surprising.

### *sui1zieng5/tang7si6* • *sui1riêng5/dang7si6* • 雖然/但是 • “although”/”but”

This can be used as a pair to form paired clauses:

> sui1zieng5 CLAUSE1, tang7si6 CLAUSE2
>
> I1 **sui1zieng5** si7 Geung6tang1-nang5, **tang7si7** ia7 oi7hiau6 tar2 Tie7tsiu1-ue7.
>
> I1 **sui1riêng5** si7 Geng6dang1-nang5, **dang7si7** ia7 oi7hiao6 dan2 Diê7ziu1-uê7.
>
> 伊 **雖然** 是 廣東儂，**但是** 也 會曉 呾 潮州話。
>
> He **although** is Canton-person, **but** also able speak Teochew.
>
> Although he’s Cantonese, he is also able to speak Teochew.

Can also be used independently:

> sui1zieng5 CLAUSE1, CLAUSE2

> CLAUSE1, tang7si6 CLAUSE2

### *puk8kue7* • *bug8guê7* • 不過 • “but”, “nonetheless”

Used similarly to *tang7si6* above.

> I1 ai2 teung6kheu2 Hiang1kang2, **puk8kue7** to7 heu2ko3 tshue7 m7tie‘4 khang1khue3.
>
> I1 ai2 deng6ke2 Hiang1gang2, **bug8guê7** do7 he2go3 cuê7 m7diêh4 kang1kuê3.
>
> 伊 愛 轉去 香港，**不過** 在 許塊 揣 唔着 工課。
>
> He want return Hong-Kong, **but** at there find NEG work.
>
> He wants to return to Hong Kong, but can’t find a job there.

### *ting1to3* • *ding1do3* • 顛倒 • “on the contrary”

> I1 bo7 sie1hu6, **ting1to2** khoi’4 nang2 zu6 tsoi7 mua7hueng5.
>
> I1 bho7 siê1hu6, **ding1do2** koih4 nang2 ru6 zoi7 mua7huêng5.
>
> 伊 無 相輔， **顛倒** 乞 俺 愈 濟 麻煩。
>
> He NEG help, **on-the-contrary** give we yet more trouble.
>
> He was of no help, but on the contrary gave us yet more trouble.

### *li6* • *li6* • 哩 • “on one hand... on the other...”

To contrast two alternative scenarios.

> A1-io6 A1-ia5 oi6, kai7 tsui6keung1 ***li6*** tsio2 tua7, kai7 ang5 ***li6*** tsio2 soi3, kheung2 tsai7tek4 lou5? Bo7piang3 mi’2!
>
> A1-io6 A1-ia5 oi6, gai7 zui6geng1 ***li6*** zio2 dua7, gai7 ang5 ***li6*** zio2 soi3, keng2 zai7dêg4 lou5? Bho7biang3 mih2!
>
> A-io 阿爺 oi， 個 水缸 ***哩*** ～ 大， 個 甕 ***哩*** ～ 細， 囥 怎得 了？ 無變 乜！
>
> Aiyoh Old-man SFP, CL water-jar on-one-hand thus big, CL pot on-one-hand thus small, insert how done? NEG-possible any!
>
> O, Old Master, the water jar is so big, but the earthern pot is so small, it can’t be possible to put one in the other.
>
> (*Knowing one but not the other* 「只知其一，不知其二」 陳四文)

Cause and effect (“because”, “thus”)
------------------------------------

### *ing1ui5/so6ih2* • *ing1ui5/so6in2* • 因為/所以 • “because”/“therefore”

This is another set of paired conjunctions.

> **ing1ui5** CAUSE, **so6ih2** EFFECT
>
> I1 **ing1ui7** teng6pai2 pak4 khoi’4 kau2 ka6, **so6ih2** king1zik8 oi7 kiar1 kau2.
>
> I1 **ing1ui7** dêng6bai2 bag4 koih4 gao2 ga6, **so6in2** ging1rig8 oi7 gian1 gao2.
>
> 伊 **因為** 頂擺 識 乞 狗 咬，**所以** 經日 會 驚 狗。
>
> He **because** past PERF PASS dog bite, **therefore** today can fear dog.

As he had been bitten by a dog in the past, he’s afraid of dogs now.

They can also be used separately.

CAUSE, **so6ih2** EFFECT

> Ua2 kiar6zik8 tshiar6ke3, **so6ih2** bo7 kheu2 tso2kang1.
>
> Ua2 gian6rig8 cian6gê3, **so6in2** bho7 ke2 zo2gang1.
>
> 我 ～日 請假， **所以** 無 去 做工。
>
> I today on-leave, **therefore** NEG go work.
>
> I’ve taken the day off, that’s why I’m not at work.

**ing1ui5** CAUSE, EFFECT

> Ua2 **ing1ui7** tshiar6ke3, king1zik8 to7 lai6.
>
> Ua2 **ing1ui7** cian6gê3, ging1rig8 do7 lai6.
>
> 我 **因為** 請假，經日 在 內。
>
> I **because** on-leave, today at inside.
>
> Because I’m on leave, I’m at home today.

EFFECT, **ing1ui5** CAUSE

> Ua2 u7 si7gang1 khiau6kha1, si7 **ing1ui7** ua2 king1zik8 bo7 tso2kang1.
>
> Ua2 u7 si7gang1 kiao6ka1, si7 **ing1ui7** ua2 ging1rig8 bho7 zo2gang1.
>
> 我 有 時間 曲腳， 是 **因為** 我 經日 無 做工。
>
> I have time leg-up, is **because** I today NEG work.
>
> I can put my feet up, because I’m not working today.

### *tsiu6* • *ziu6* • 就 • “therefore”, “then”

CAUSE, tsiu6 EFFECT

> Ua2 tou2khung3, **tsiu7** kheu2 tshue7 tsiak4.
>
> Ua2 dou2kung3, **ziu7** ke2 cuê7 ziag4.
>
> 我 肚困， **就** 去 揣 嚼。
>
> I hungry, **then** go find eat.
>
> I’ll go find something to eat when I’m hungry.

*tsiu6* can also mean “thereafter”, indicating a sequence of events that do not necessarily have a cause-and-effect relationship (see below).

The preposition *ui7liau3* 為了 “so that” can also express intention:

> **Ui7liau3** kiar6sung1 kai7 bue7lai5, i1 tseng2-liau7 tsi2-tsang7 tshiu7.
>
> **Ui7liao3** gian6sung1 gai7 bhuê7lai5, i1 zêng2-liao7 zi2-zang7 ciu7.
>
> **為了** 囝孫 個 未來， 伊 種了 只 欉 樹。
>
> **For** children-grandchildren future, he plant-PERF this CL tree.
>
> For his children’s sake, he planted this tree.

### *tsiar3* • *zian3* • 正 • “only then”, “thereafter” (adverb)

The Hokkien cognate is written 才, borrowing the character for an equivalent word in Mandarin. This is not strictly speaking a conjunction or preposition but is an adverb.



### *liau2* • *liao2* • 了 • “so”, “and then”

Similar to English “So, …” or “After that, ….” In the following example, the first *liau2* is like English “and then”, whereas the second *liau2* is a perfective aspect marker modifying the verb *huak8tshuk4* (see [“Aspect”](pages/aspect.md)).

> **Liau2**, i1 huak8tshuk4 liau7 tsik4kai7 por2kau3
>
> **Liao2**, i1 huag8cug4 liao7 zig4gai7 bon2gao3
>
> **了**，伊 發出 了 一個 佈告
>
> **Then**, he publish-PERF one-CL report
>
> And then, \[the auditors\] issued a report
>
> [*Speech by Low Thia Khiang*](texts/low_thia_khiang.md)


### *zu2* • *ru2* • 愈 • “the more that X, the more Y”

When increasing or intensifying something causes something else to increase too. The word **zu2** 愈 is also used in comparisons, typically in the word *zu6keng2* 愈更 (see [“Comparisons, Quantities, and Degrees”](pages/comparisons.md)).

> i1 thau2 **zu6** li7hai7, heu6kai7-nang5-kai7 mi6hiuh5 mau1 **zu6**-king1, bo7 tui6 **zu6**-ke6
>
> i1 tao2 **ru6** li7hai7, he6gai7-nang5-gai7 mi6hiun5 mao1 **ru6**-ging1, bho7 dui6 **ru6**-gê5
>
> 伊 透 **愈** 厲害， 許個儂個 棉裘 包 **愈** 緊， 帽 戴 **愈** 底
>
> he blow **more** strongly, that-person-GEN coat wrap **more** tightly, hat wear **more** low
>
> the stronger it [the North Wind] blew, the tighter the man wrapped his coat around himself, and the lower he wore his cap
>
> [*The North Wind and the Sun*](texts/aesop.md)

Conditionals and conjecture (“if”, “maybe”)
-------------------------------------------

### *na7si6* • *na7si6* • 哪是 – “if”

Also written as 若是, using the character 若, which means “if” in Mandarin and literary Chinese, for its semantic value. Also pronounced *a1si7* 阿是.

It is frequently paired with *tsiu6* 就 to mean “if …, then …”:

> **na7si6** CLAUSE1, **tsiu6** CLAUSE2
>
> Leu2 **na7si7** bo7 tshiu2khi2, ih6au6 tsiu7 oi7 hou7hue3.
>
> Le2 **na7si7** bho7 ciu2ki2, in6ao6 ziu7 oi7 hou7huê3.
>
> 汝 **哪是** 無 漱齒， 以後 就 會 後悔。
>
> You **if** NEG brush-teeth, after then will regret.
>
> If you don’t brush your teeth, you’ll regret it.

*Na7si6* can also be used on its own:

> **na7si6** CLAUSE1, CLAUSE2
>
> **Na7si7** bo7 a1ma5 kai7 tsih1thi5, ua2 boi7tek8 u7 king1zik8-kai7 seng7tsiu6.
>
> **Na7si7** bho7 a1ma5 gai7 zin1ti5, ua2 bhoi7dêg8 u7 ging1rig8-gai7 sêng7ziu6.
>
> **哪是** 無 阿媽 個 支持，我 無得 有 經日 個 成就。
>
> **If** NEG mother-GEN support, I NEG-able have today-GEN success.
>
> Without my mother’s support, I wouldn’t have the success I have today.

Often compounded in the set phrase *na7si7tsie2ser1* 哪是照生 “if so”:

> **Na7si7tsie2ser1**, ua2 tsiu7 mai6-bue2!
>
> **Na7si7ziê2sên1**, ua2 ziu7 mai6-bhuê2!
>
> **哪是照生**， 我 就 莫 買！
>
> **If-so**, I thus NEG buy!
>
> If that’s the case, I’m not buying!

### *teu7hui1* • *de7hui1* • 除非 • “unless”

Similar to *na7si7*, but with the converse meaning.

> **teu7hui1** CLAUSE1, CLAUSE2
>
> **Teu7hui1** i1 khoi’4 tsih1 haih5 ua7, ua2 mai2 sie1hu6 i7.
>
> **De7hui1** i1 koih4 zin1 hain5 ua7, ua2 mai2 siê1hu6 i7.
>
> **除非** 伊 乞 錢 還 我， 我 莫 相輔 伊。
>
> **Unless** he give money return I, I NEG help he.
>
> Unless he returns me the money, I won’t help him.

### *tsek8/tsiu6* • *zêg8/ziu6* • 一/就 • “when / then”

This construction has the meaning “the moment X occurs, then Y”. This event or occurrence X that triggers Y can be in the past or future, or recurring.

> Lau6seu1 **tsek4** kiar5 zip8lai7, hak4seng1 **tsiu7** kuer1tshui3.
>
> Lao6se1 **zêg4** gian5 rib8lai7, hag4sêng1 **ziu7** guên1cui3.
>
> 老師 **一** 行 入來， 學生 **就** 關嘴。
>
> Teacher **one** walk inwards, student **then** shut-mouth.
>
> The moment that the teacher came in, the students stopped talking.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> Ngiau6tsheu2 **tsek4** tsau2 tshuk4lai7, nang2 **tsiu7** ai2 mer6mer2 kheu2 lia’8 i7!
>
> Ngiao6ce2 **zêg4** zao2 cug4lai7, nang2 **ziu7** ai2 mên6mên2 ke2 liah8 i7!
>
> 貓鼠 **一** 走 出來， 俺 **就** 愛 猛猛 去 掠 伊！
>
> Rat **one** run outward, we-incl. **then** ought quickly go catch it!
>
> The moment that the rat runs out, we should quickly grab it!

### *an2ne1* • *an2nê1* • 按呢 • “in that case”

> Leu2 ai2 tsiak4 ou7ni5, **an2ne1** tie’4 ka1ki7 o’4 tso2ni7 tso3.
>
> Le2 ai2 ziag4 ou7ni5, **an2nê1** diêh4 ga1gi7 oh4 zo2ni7 zo3.
>
> 汝 愛 嚼 芋泥， **按呢** 着 家己 學 做呢 做。
>
> You want eat yam-paste, **in-that-case** should self learn how make.
>
> You want to eat yam paste? Then learn how to make it yourself.

*an2ne1* can also function as an adverb, meaning “such”, “to such an extent”.

> Leu2 tso2ni7 **an2ne1** hek8?
>
> Le2 zo2ni7 **an2nê1** hêg8?
>
> 汝 做呢 **按呢** 乏？
>
> You why **so** tired?
>
> Why are you so tired?

*m6li1* 唔哩 is another conjunction that also means “in that case”.
