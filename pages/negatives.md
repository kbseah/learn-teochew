Negatives with *m6* 唔 and *bo5* 無
-----------------------------------

### *m6* • *m6* • 唔

*m6* 唔 cannot stand by itself but must always be attached to another word to negate it. It is always used as a prefix. *M6* is used to negate the following classes of words:

#### Negating *si6* 是 “to be”

The copula *si6* 唔 is negated by *m6*: *m7si6* 唔是. (See [“Auxiliary verbs”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/auxiliary_verbs.md %}).)

> Ua2 ***m7si7*** zik4pung6-nang5.
>
> Ua2 ***m7si7*** rig4bung6-nang5.
>
> 我 ***唔是*** 日本人。
>
> I ***NEG-COP*** Japan-person.
>
> I am not Japanese.

#### Negating stative verbs

Stative verbs describe states of being, in contrast to dynamic verbs, which describe actions and events. The majority of verbs are dynamic verbs. Stative verbs are relatively few and have to do mostly with thinking, beliefs, and opinion.

> *m7-pak8* • *m7-bag8* • 唔識 • “not-recognize/know”
>
> *m7-ki1tek4* • *m7-gi1dêg4* • 唔記得 • “not-remember”

#### Negating ungradeable adjectives

Ungradeable adjectives describe properties that cannot be placed on a sliding scale. For example, one is either ignorant of danger (*tsai1si2* 知死) or not (*m7tsai1si2* 唔知死); it is impossible to be half-*tsai1si2*.

> I1 ou1 bo7 mue’4kiar6, ***m7***-kam1nguang6!
>
> I1 ou1 bho7 muêh4gian6, ***m7***-gam1nguang6!
>
> 伊 污 無 物仔， ***唔***-甘願！
>
> He dig no thing, ***NEG*** satisfied!
>
> He was not satisfied because he could not dig up anything [to use against us]!
>
> [“Speech by Low Thia Khiang”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/texts/low_thia_khiang.md %})
>
> \*\*\*
>
> “Le kha’4-kue2 ***m7***-tseu7liang6 lo2!”
>
> “Le2 kah4-guê2 ***m7***-ze7liang6 lo2!”
>
> “汝 𥑐-過 ***唔***-自量 囉！”
>
> You too-much ***NEG***-self-aware PERF!
>
> You don’t know your own strength!
>
> [“North Wind and the Sun”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/texts/aesop.md %})

Gradeable adjectives, on the other hand, are negated with *bhoi6* 袂 (see below).

### *bo5* • *bho5* • 無

*bo5* is the negative form of the auxiliary verb *u6* 有 (see [“Auxiliary verbs”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/auxiliary_verbs.md %})).

#### Negative of u6 有

Sentences with *u6* 有 can be negated simply by substituting *bo5* 無. Here are examples for the different functions of *u6*.

> [Possession]
>
> Ua2 ***bo7*** tsih5.
>
> Ua2 ***bho7*** zin5.
>
> 我 ***無*** 錢。
>
> I ***NEG*** money.
>
> I have no money.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> [Existence]
>
> Ua2 tsih7to7-toih2 tsek4-kai7 tung6kiar2 a1-***bo5***.
>
> Ua2 zin7do7-doin2 zêg4-gai7 dung6gian2 a1-***bho5***.
>
> 我 錢袋底 一個 盾仔 也 ***無***。
>
> I wallet-inside one-CL coin also-***NEG***.
>
> I don’t have a single coin in my wallet.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> [Affirmative and habitual]
>
> Ua2 ***bo7*** pua’4tsih5! Ua2 tsek4-seng1 ia7 ***bo7*** pua’4 kue3.
>
> Ua2 ***bho7*** buah4zin5! Ua2 zêg4-sêng1 ia7 ***bho7*** buah4-guê3.
>
> 我 ***無*** ～錢！ 我 一生 也 ***無*** ～過。
>
> I ***NEG*** gamble! I entire-life also ***NEG*** gamble-EXP.
>
> I don’t gamble! I have never gambled my entire life.

#### Negating *to6* 在 • location or progressive aspect

*bo5* is used as a prefix to negate *to6* 在 “at”, even though the positive form does not require *u6* 有.

> I1 to7 tshu2lai6. Ua2 ***bo7-to7*** tshu2lai6.
>
> I1 do7 cu2lai6. Ua2 ***bho7-do7*** cu2lai6.
>
> 伊 在 厝內。 我 ***無在*** 厝內。
>
> He/she at home. I ***NEG-at*** home.
>
> He/she is at home. I am not at home.

*to6* is also used as a progressive aspect marker, to indicate an action that is ongoing.

> Ua2 ***bo7-to7*** thak8kiu5.
>
> Ua2 ***bho7-do7*** tag8giu5.
>
> 我 ***無在*** 踢球。
>
> I ***NEG-PROG*** kick-ball.
>
> I am not playing ball.


Fused negatives
---------------

Many negative words begin with either the *m* or *b* sound. Given that the basic negative prefix is *m6* 唔, it is likely that they have evolved from fusion of *m6* + the positive form, especially in the case of auxiliary verbs:

> m6 唔 + oi6 會 = boi6 𠁞/袂
>
> m6 唔 + ai3 愛 = mai3 免
>
> m6 唔 + ho2 好 = mo2 孬

A few are not modal verbs:

> m6 唔 + ui3 畏 = mui ～ • “do not worry”
>
> m6 唔 + ek4 憶 = mek4 ～ • mek8tek4 ～得 “forget”


Negatives of modal verbs
------------------------

Modal verbs have special negative forms. Most of these appear to be derived from fusion of *m6* and the original modal verb, e.g. *m6* 唔 + *oi6* 會 (*boi6* 𠁞/袂), *m6* + *ho2* 好 (*mo2* 孬). Usage is straightforward, being just like the respective positive modal verbs, which are further explained in the section [“Modality”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/auxiliary_verbs.md %}).

### *boi6* • *bhoi6* • 𠁞 or 袂 - ability, “unable to”

Negative of *oi6* 會

> Ua2 boi7 pha’4 mua7tsia’4.
>
> Ua2 bhoi7 pah4 mua7ziah4.
>
> 我 袂 拍 麻雀。
>
> I cannot play mahjong.
>
> I’m unable to play mahjong.

It is also used to negate compounds that are derived from *oi6*:

> *boi7hiau2* • *bhoi7hiao2* • 袂曉 • unable (knowledge, skills)

> *boi7tek8* • *bhoi7dêg8* • 袂得 • unable (capacity, physical ability)

> *boi7sai2* • *bhoi7sai2* • 袂使 • unable (permission, capacity)

The usage *boi7sai2* 袂使 comes from Hokkien, and is common in overseas Teochew (see [“Loan words”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/overseas.md %})). The Teochew equivalent is *mo2* 孬.

#### Negating gradeable adjectives

In addition to being a negative modal verb, *boi6* is also used to negate gradeable adjectives (in contrast to *m6* 唔 for ungradeable adjectives). These adjectives describe attributes that can be compared on a scale, e.g. “more beautiful”, “less intelligent”.

> Ua2 ***boi7*** tshong1meng5.
>
> Ua2 ***bhoi7*** cong1mêng5.
>
> 我 ***袂*** 聰明。
>
> I ***not*** intelligent.
>
> I’m not intelligent.

### *mo2* • *mo2* • 孬

Negative of *ho2* 好 and *kho6i2* 可以 – permission

> Leu2 mo6 zip8lai7.
>
> Le2 mo6 rib8lai7.
>
> 汝 孬 入來。
>
> You NEG enter.
>
> Do not come in.

Negative of *ai3* 愛 – intention, obligation

> Khang1khue3 mo6 si2sua2 tso3.
>
> Kang1kuê3 mo6 si2sua2 zo3.
>
> 工課 孬 四散 做。
>
> Work NEG slipshod do.
>
> Work should not be done carelessly.

As an adjective or adverb meaning “bad” (negative of *ho2* 好), in words like *m7mo2* 無孬 or *bhoi7mo2* 袂孬 (“not bad”), or *siang1mo2* 相孬 (“break off a friendship”, negative of *siang1ho2* 相好).

> I1 tso7-kai7 ou7ni5 bi7sou3 ***bhoi7mo2***.
>
> I1 zo7-gai7 ou7ni5 bhi7sou3 ***boi7mo2***.
>
> 伊 做個 芋泥 味素 ***袂孬***。
>
> He cook-REL yam-paste flavor ***not-bad***.
>
> The yam paste that he made is not bad.

### *mai3* • *mai3* • 莫

Negative of *ai3* 愛 – intention, obligation

> I1 mai2 thiar1ue7.
>
> I1 mai2 tian1uê7.
>
> 伊 莫 聽話。
>
> He NEG listen.
>
> He does not want to listen [to advice].

### *meng2* • *mêng2* • 免 • necessity, “don’t need to”

Negative of *tie’4* 着 - obligation

> *meng6khe’8khi3* • *mêng6kêh8ki3* • 免客氣 • “don’t stand on ceremony”

Compared to *mo2* 孬, *meng2* is a gentler way of declining an offer.

### *bo7piang3* • *bho7biang3* • 無變 • circumstances, “unable to”

Negative of *u7piang3* 有變

> Ua2 phua2per3, ***bo7piang2*** tshuk8mung5.
>
> Ua2 pua2ben3, ***bho7biang2*** cug8mung5.
>
> 我 破病， ***無變*** 出門。
>
> I ill, ***NEG-able*** go-out.
>
> I’m sick and unable to leave the house.

### *bue7* • *bhuê7* • 未 • expectation, “not yet”

Negative of *ai3* 愛 - “will”

> Nang2 tang2 puar6zik8 lou7, hue1tshia1 huar1 bue7 lai5.
>
> Nang2 dang2 buan6rig8 lou7, huê1cia1 huan1 bhuê7 lai5.
>
> 俺 等 半日 囉， 火車 還 未 來。
>
> We wait half-day PERF, train still NEG come.
>
> We’ve been waiting half the day, but the train has still not arrived.
