Auxiliary verbs
===============
{: .no_toc}

Auxiliary verbs are verbs that cannot stand by themselves, but which “help” other verbs by adding functional meaning. They usually have to be combined with a main verb in a sentence. Examples of auxiliary verbs in English are “to be”, “have”, “can”, “should”. In Teochew, the auxiliary verbs can similarly be classified by their function into primary auxiliaries (“be”, “have”) and modal verbs (e.g. “should”, “may”).

*si6* • *si6* • 是 • “to be”, the copula
----------------------------------------

*si6* 是 is cognate to Mandarin *shì* and has similar usage in Teochew.

It is used to join together two nouns or noun phrases to mean that they are the same or identical (“to be”, “is”). In this usage it is known as the copula verb.

> [Noun1] + si6 是 + [Noun2]
>
> I1 ***si7*** tang1kim1 tsong6li2.
>
> I1 ***si7*** dang1gim1 zong6li2.
>
> 伊 ***是*** 當今 總理。
>
> He/she ***is*** present prime-minister.
>
> He/she is the sitting Prime Minister.

It is negated by the prefix *m6* 唔: *m7si6* 唔是 (see [“Negatives”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/negatives.md %})).

By itself, it is used to reply “yes” to a yes-no question (see [“Questions”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/questions.md %})):

> I1 si7m7si7 tang1kim1 tsong6li2? • Si6!
>
> I1 si7m7si7 dang1gim1 zong6li2? • Si6!
>
> 伊 是唔是 當今 總理？ • 是！
>
> He/she is-NEG-is present prime-minister? • Is!
>
> Is he/she the sitting Prime Minister? Yes [he/she is]!

*u6* • *u6* • 有 • “to have”
----------------------------

*u6* can be both a main verb and an auxiliary. Its negative form is *bo6* 無 (see [“Negatives”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/negatives.md %})).

### Possession

*u6* can function as a main verb meaning possession of something.

> Ua2 ***u7*** tsek4-tsia’8 tshia1.
>
> Ua2 ***u7*** zêg4-ziah8 cia1.
>
> 我 ***有*** 一隻 車。
>
> I ***have*** one-CL car.
>
> I have a motorcar.

### Existence

It can also mean the existence of something, usually in the context of a time or place.

> [Time or place] - *u6* 有 - [Noun phrase]
>
> Tshia1-toih2 ***u7*** nang5.
>
> Cia1-doin2 ***u7*** nang5
>
> 車低 ***有*** 儂。
>
> Car-inside ***have*** person.
>
> There is/are person(s) in the car.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> E7-gue’8 ***u7*** no7-zik8 kiar6khi5.
>
> Ê7-ghueh8 ***u7*** no7-rig8 gian6ki5.
>
> 下月 ***有*** 兩日 假期。
>
> Next-month ***have*** two-day holiday.
>
> There are two holidays next month.

### Affirmative aspect

*u6* 有 is added in front of another verb, when the speaker wants to affirm that the action has indeed occurred or has been completed. This is known as the affirmative aspect, or perfect aspect (see [“Aspect and time”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/aspect.md %})).

> Tsong6li2 king1zik8 ***u7*** tsiak4 ou7ni5.
>
> Zong6li2 ging1rig8 ***u7*** ziag4 ou7ni5.
>
> 總理 經日 ***有*** 嚼 芋泥
>
> Prime-minister today ***have*** eat yam-paste.
>
> The Prime Minister has eaten yam paste today.

In contrast, without *u6*, the sentence above could mean that the prime minister is presently eating yam paste, or plans to eat yam paste (but has not yet done so).

### Habitual aspect

*u6* can also prefix verbs to mean that these actions are done habitually.

> Ua2 ***u7*** tsiak4hung1. Ua2 ***u7*** lim1 tsiu2. Ua2 bo7 phak8 bou2.
>
> Ua2 ***u7*** ziag4hung1. Ua2 ***u7*** lim1 ziu2. Ua2 bho7 pag8 bhou2.
>
> 我 ***有*** 吃煙。 我 ***有*** 啉 酒。 我 無 拍 𡚸。
>
> I ***have*** smoke. I ***have*** drink wine. I NEG beat wife.
>
> I smoke. I drink. I don’t beat my wife.

Modal verbs
-----------

“Modality” refers to probability, necessity, permission, and related concepts. English has a special class of verbs called modal verbs, such as “can”, “should”, “may”, and “must”. Like other auxiliary verbs, they must be combined with a main verb. The exception is when the meaning is already implicitly understood:

> Q: **Must** I?
>
> A: Yes you **must** \[brush your teeth\].

Modality can also be expressed through adverbs, e.g. “certainly”, “surely” (see below).

One characteristic of modal verbs in Teochew and Southern Min langauges is that many of them have special negative forms (see [“Negatives”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/negatives.md %})).

Modal verbs usually come before the main verb that they modify. Here they are listed alongside their corresponding negative forms.

### oi6 會 / boi6 袂 – “can”, “able”, “will”

Teochew *oi6* is similar to its Mandarin cognate *huì* 會. It has a
number of overlapping functions; what they have in common is that they
refer to actions or events that lie in the future.

“Can cause or result in something”

> I1 u7 lim1 ko1pi5 tsiar2 **oi7** tsher2.
>
> I1 u7 lim1 go1bi5 zian2 **oi7** cên2.
>
> 伊 有 啉 咖啡 正 **會** 醒。
>
> He PERF drink coffee then **can** awake.
>
> He is only alert after drinking coffee.

“Able to do something”

> I1 ngou7hue7 zu7 **oi7** sek8 teung7nang7zi7.
>
> I1 ngou7huê7 zu7 **oi7** sêg8 deng7nang7ri7.
>
> 伊 五歲 就 **會** 識 唐儂字。
>
> He five-years-old already **can** read Chinese-characters.
>
> He could read Chinese characters at the age of five.

“Something will occur”

> Hue1tshia1 lak4tiam2puar7 tsiar2 **oi7** lai5.
>
> Huê1cia1 lag4diam2buan7 zian2 **oi7** lai5.
>
> 火車 六點半 正 **會** 來。
>
> Train six-thirty then **will** come.
>
> The train will only arrive at six thirty.

### Derivatives of *oi6*

#### oi7hiau2 會曉 – “can”, “able”, “know how to”

Used when referring to ability in the sense of skill or know-how. Compare to
*oi7tek4* and *u7piang3* below.

> I1 **oi7hiau6** eng7 seung2puar5 seung2sieu3.
>
> I1 **oi7hiao6** êng7 seng2buan5 seng2siêu3.
>
> 伊 **會曉** 用 算盤 算數。
>
> He **able** use abacus calculate.
>
> He knows how to calculate with the abacus.

Unlike *oi6* itself and the other derivatives of *oi6*, *oi7hiau2*
cannot be used to indicate a future event:

> \*I1 muar2zik8 **oi7hiau6** lai7 seung2 mang7kiu5.
>
> \*I1 muan2zig8 **oi7hiao6** lai7 seng2 mang7giu5.
>
> \*伊 ～日 **會曉** 來 耍 網球。
>
> \*He tomorrow **able** come play tennis.

#### oi7tek4 – 會得 – “can”, “able”

> I1 **oi7tek8** to7 tshek4 hung1tseng1 lai6 tsau6 nor7 kung1li2.
>
> I1 **oi7dêg8** do7 cêk4 hung1zêng1 lai6 zao2 non7 gung1li2.
>
> 伊 **會得** 在 七 分鐘 內 走 兩 公里。
>
> He **able** in seven minutes within run two kilometers.
>
> He can run two kilometers in seven minutes.

For the above example, *oi7hiau2* would not be appropriate, as
*oi7hiau2* refers to ability in the sense of know-how, rather than a
purely physical skill.

#### oi7sai2 – 會使 – “can”, “may”

This can have the meaning of “may” in the sense of having permission:

> Ua2 **oi7sai6** eng7 leu2-kai7 seung2puar5 tsek4e7 a1-boi6?
>
> Ua2 **oi7sai6** êng7 le2gai7 seng2buan5 zêg4ê7 a1-bhoi6?
>
> 我 **會使** 用 汝個 算盤 一下 a袂？
>
> I **may** use you-GEN abacus DEL or-not?
>
> May I use your abacus for a while?

Both *oi7tek4* and *oi7sai2* can mean “can” in the sense of “being in a
position to do something”:

> I1 muar2zik8 **oi7tek8** / **oi7sai6** lai7 seung2 mang7kiu5.
>
> I1 muan2zig8 **oi7dêg8** / **oi7sai6** lai7 seng2 mang7giu5.
>
> 伊 ～日 **會得** / **會使** 來 耍 網球。
>
> He tomorrow **can** come play tennis.
>
> He can come to play tennis tomorrow.

### kho6ih2 可以 / m7kho6ih2 唔可以 – “may”, “can”

Comparable to its Mandarin cognate *kěyǐ*, this can mean either
permission (like *oi7sai2*) or ability (like *oi7tek8*).

### u7piang3 有變 / bo7piang3 無變 – “can”, “able”

This is used when referring to ability in the sense of circumstances (“in a
position to do something”). Compare to *oi7hiau2* and *oi7sai2* above.

> I1 u7piang2 ka’4 nang2 tso2 kang1khue3 a1-bo5?
>
> I1 u7biang2 gah4 nang2 zo2 gang1kuê3 a1-bho5?
>
> 伊 **有變** 佮 俺 做 工課 a無？
>
> He able for we-incl. do work or-not?
>
> Is he able to do a job for us?

### ai3 愛 / mai3 莫 – “want”, “will”

> Ua2 ai2 sang2 tsi2tiou7 heu5 khoi’4 leu3.
>
> Ua2 ai2 sang2 zi2diou7 he5 koih4 le3.
>
> 我 **愛** 送 只條 魚 乞 汝。
>
> I want present this-CL fish give you.
>
> I want to give you this fish as a gift.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> I1 mai2 ka’4 uang2 tso2 pheng7iou2.
>
> I1 mai2 gah4 uang2 zo2 peng7iu2.
>
> 伊 **莫** 佮 阮 做 朋友。
>
> He not-want with us-excl. do friend.
>
> He doesn’t want to befriend us.

### tie’8 着 / meng2 免 – “should”, “must”

Among other functions (see [“Verbal complements”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/verbal_complements.md %})), tie’8 着 has the modal meanings of “should” or
“must”.

> Nang2 e7loi6pai3 **tie’4** ga’8 A1-Meng5 tsiak4 tsa6tshang1.
>
> Nang2 ê7loi6pai3 **diêh4** gah8 A1-Mêng5 ziag4 za6cang1.
>
> 俺 下禮拜 **着** 佮 阿明 嚼 早餐。
>
> We-incl. next-week **should** with A-Meng eat breakfast.
>
> We should have breakfast with Ah Meng next week.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> Ua2 muar2zik8 **meng6** kheu2 hak4hau6.
>
> Ua2 muan2zig8 **mêng6** ke2 hag4hao6.
>
> 我 ～日 **免** 去 學校。
>
> I tomorrow **need-not** go school.
>
> I don’t have school tomorrow.

#### tie’4ai3 – diêh4ai3 – 着愛 – “should”, “must”

> Nang2 **tie’4ai2** zik4zik8 tso2 tam7po’4 ung7dong6.
>
> Nang2 **diêh4ai2** rig4rig8 zo2 dam7boh4 ung7dong6.
>
> 俺 **着愛** 日日 做 淡簿 運動。
>
> We **ought** daily do a-little-bit exercise.
>
> We ought to do a little bit of exercise every day.

### eng1kai1 應該 – “should”

Comparable to the Mandarin cognate *yīnggāi*.

> Nang2 **eng1gai1** zik4zik8 tso2 tam7po’4 ung7dong6.
>
> Nang2 **êng1gai1** zig4zig8 zo2 dam7boh4 ung7dong6.
>
> 俺 **應該** 日日 做 淡簿 運動。
>
> We **should** daily do a-bit exercise.
>
> We should do a little bit of exercise every day.

### ho2 好 / mo2 孬 – “may”, “should”

This uses the colloquial pronunciation of 好, compare with *hauh3*
below.

> Leu2 tso2ho2 kong1khue3 tsiar2 **ho6 **kheu3.
>
> Le2 zo2ho2 gong1kuê3 zian2 **ho6** ke3.
>
> 汝 做好 功課 正 **好** 去。
>
> You do-finished homework then **may** go.
>
> You may leave when you’ve finished your homework.
>
> \*\*\*
>
> Tsie’4ser1 uar3 lou7, ning2 **ho6** teung2tshu3 lou7.
>
> Ziêh4sên1 uan3 lou7, ning2 **ho6** deng2cu3 lou7.
>
> 照生 晏 𡀔，恁 **好** 轉厝 𡀔。
>
> So late PRF, you-all **should** go-home PRF.
>
> It’s already so late, you should all go home.

### hauh3 好 / m7hauh3 唔好 – “willing to”

This uses the literary pronunciation of 好, compare with *ho2* above.

> Ning2 **hauh5-m7hau5** sie1hu6 tsek4e7?
>
> Ning2 **haon5-m7haon5** siê1hu6 zêg4ê7?
>
> 恁 **好唔好** 相輔 一下？
>
> You-pl. **willing-NEG-willing** help somewhat?
>
> Are you willing to help out a bit?

The example above is a yes-no question following the A-NOT-A pattern
(see [“Questions”]({{ site.baseurl }}{% link pages/questions.md %})).

> Leu2 tso2ni7 sui7si5 tsiu7 tsie2ser1 **m7hauh3**?
>
> Le2 zo2ni7 sui7si5 ziu7 ziê2sên1 **m7haon3**?
>
> 汝 做呢 隨時 就 照生 **唔好**？
>
> You why suddenly thus like-this **unwilling**?
>
> Why are you suddenly so unwilling?

### Modal adverbs

Modality can also be expressed through adverbs.

> *pa1lak4* • *ba1lag4* • ～～ • “maybe” (derived from Malay *pahala*)
>
> *kho2leng5* • *ko2lêng5* • 可能 • “maybe, possibly”
>
> *tiar7tie’8* • *dian7diêh8* • 定着 • “certainly”
>
> *bue7si7* • *bhuê7si7* • 未是 • “surely, could it be”
